Olympique de Marseille, also known simply as Marseille or by the abbreviation OM, is a French professional football club based in Marseille that competes in Ligue 1, the top flight of French football. Founded in 1899, the club has won nine league titles, ten Coupe de France titles, three Coupe de la Ligue titles, and three Trophée des Champions titles. In 1993, coach Raymond Goethals led the team to become the first and only French club to win the UEFA Champions League, defeating AC Milan 1–0 in the final, and in the same year became one of three French clubs to win a major UEFA competition. Marseille has the second-most Ligue 1 titles behind Paris Saint-Germain.
